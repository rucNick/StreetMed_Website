Our Mission

Street Medicine at Pitt exists to bridge the gap between traditional healthcare and the most vulnerable members of our community. We believe that everyone deserves access to compassionate, dignified medical care.

Through interdisciplinary collaboration and community partnerships, we work to eliminate barriers to healthcare while training the next generation of healthcare providers to understand and address health equity.

Areas of Focus

Our work is centered around four key pillars to provide holistic support to our community.

  • Education

    • Educate our campus and city on the realities of homelessness—and show clear, practical ways anyone can help.

  • Deliver consistent, relationship-centered care

    • Conduct regular street rounds in Downtown and East Liberty, offering basic medical assessments, wound care, and harm-reduction supplies.
    • Track repeat encounters so clients see familiar faces and build trust.

  • Connect clients to long-term services

    • Create a streamlined referral pathway to local free clinics, mental health providers, and housing programs.
    • Follow up after every referral to confirm the client reached the service or identify barriers.

  • Provide on-scene social support

    • Distribute hygiene kits, weather-appropriate clothing, snacks, and naloxone.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is homelessness? ▼

Homelessness is when someone doesn't have a steady, safe place to live and instead has to sleep in shelters, cars, or outside until they can afford a home.

How does it affect health? ▼

Higher rates of wounds, infections, untreated chronic diseases, mental-health crises, and exposure injuries. Life expectancy is 12 years shorter than the housed population.

Homelessness & Health: What's the Connection?

Why don't more people use shelters? ▼

Limited beds, strict rules (curfews, sobriety, no pets/partners), safety concerns, crowding, lack of privacy, and past trauma push some to camp or stay in vehicles instead.
